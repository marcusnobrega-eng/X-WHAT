Abstract

Cost-effective water distribution network design is crucial for the management of drinking water in cities. This paper introduces an Excel tool for designing cost-effective water distribution networks with satisfactory pressure performance. Unlike traditional methods like Hardy-Cross, this tool utilizes Excel’s built-in solver without needing conservative initial estimates or iterative corrections. We provide an open-source solution for simulating hydraulic networks, suitable for educational purposes as well. The paper outlines the mathematical framework considering network topology, mass and energy conservation, and costs associated with tank materials and pumping energy. It emphasizes its suitability for teaching complex concepts without requiring coding expertise. The tool allows a rapid sensitivity analysis, enabling users to assess the effects of varying demands or configurations on both cost and network performance. Three practical examples, including a comparison with EPANET and literature results, demonstrate its effectiveness. Additionally, we propose a tentative class organization and a unit design, following the Understanding by Design (UbD) approach, to facilitate the tool’s application in engineering education. Through optimization, the tool achieved a substantial cost reduction of 151,790 USD (9.8% of total cost) compared to standard design in a network serving 44,416 people. Supplementary material offers a detailed guide on utilizing the tool effectively.
